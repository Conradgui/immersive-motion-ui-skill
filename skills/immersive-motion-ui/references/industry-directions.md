# Industry Directions

Use this after choosing the primary register and before locking visual direction. This is a task-first decision system, not a catalog of fixed industry styles.

## Selection Protocol

1. Name the user's job-to-be-done and primary register.
2. Name the lazy default the category invites.
3. Choose the closest task family below, then generate both directions.
4. Compare the directions against content density, trust, differentiation, available assets, motion budget, accessibility, and the existing stack.
5. Select one direction with a reason. Keep the other as a rejected alternative, not a hidden fallback.
6. State which evidence would make you switch directions.

The family is not a fixed mapping from industry to color, font, theme, or engine. 用户举例是方向证据，不是永久限制。When the brief crosses families, the job-to-be-done decides.

## 1. SaaS / 产品工具

### Representative sectors

AI products, developer tools, security, fintech operations, healthcare operations, legal/insurance workflows, CRM, internal tools, and productivity software.

### Default trap

Centered marketing hero, purple glow, floating dashboard card, vague automation claims, and an app shell that sacrifices scan speed for spectacle.

### Direction A

**Operational clarity**: treat the interface as a working instrument. Use a stable shell, visible hierarchy, compact filters, deterministic states, and one restrained accent tied to status or action. Let real workflows and data carry credibility.

### Direction B

**Product proof narrative**: keep the app usable, but expose one real interaction as the signature move: a query resolving, a timeline assembling, a risk path narrowing, or a before/after workflow. The proof is specific behavior, not an atmospheric mockup.

### Decision variables

- Repeated daily use versus first-time evaluation.
- Data density, task frequency, and number of user roles.
- Whether trustworthy real product states exist.
- Existing component system and navigation model.
- Compliance, error recovery, and auditability needs.

### Motion ceiling

Low inside repeated workflows; medium for onboarding, state transitions, and product proof. Continuous background motion must never compete with tables, forms, alerts, or keyboard work.

### Avoid when

The main job is editorial storytelling, a single launch moment, direct product purchase, or an immersive event. Route those jobs to another family even if the company calls itself SaaS.

### Verification focus

Task completion, keyboard path, loading/empty/error states, table and filter stability, responsive density, truthful metrics, and no layout shift from dynamic data.

## 2. 品牌 / 作品集

### Representative sectors

Agencies, architecture, hospitality, wellness, luxury goods, craft, studios, creators, professional services, institutions, and early-stage brand launches.

### Default trap

Oversized centered statement, generic awards strip, interchangeable project cards, decorative grain, and a dark cinematic treatment that could belong to any premium brand.

### Direction A

**Editorial authority**: build a point of view through typography, sequencing, image selection, captions, and contrast between long and short rhythms. Make the first viewport a thesis about the subject, not a generic claim of excellence.

### Direction B

**Object or process as identity**: let a material, product detail, spatial system, craft step, or documented transformation become the visual grammar. Repeat that grammar across navigation, section transitions, and proof.

### Decision variables

- Strength and originality of available imagery or objects.
- Whether reputation comes from work, place, founder, process, or cultural position.
- Amount of narrative content and project evidence.
- Need for inquiry, booking, press, recruitment, or portfolio browsing.
- Existing brand assets that must be protected.

### Motion ceiling

Medium by default; high only when motion expresses a real brand behavior and the content remains inspectable. Photography-led work usually needs lighter motion than generative scenes.

### Avoid when

Users primarily operate a tool, compare inventory, complete checkout, or analyze live data. Brand atmosphere may influence those surfaces but should not become the primary family.

### Verification focus

First-viewport specificity, image fidelity, typography across breakpoints, project discoverability, inquiry path, reduced motion, and whether removing the logo still leaves a recognizable subject.

## 3. Commerce

### Representative sectors

Fashion, beauty, food, furniture, consumer hardware, automotive, travel/booking, subscriptions, marketplaces, ticketing, and premium services.

### Default trap

A campaign film hiding product facts, oversized lifestyle imagery before price or availability, identical product tiles, fake urgency, and a checkout flow styled as a brand poster.

### Direction A

**Inspection first**: prioritize image truth, variants, dimensions, materials, availability, delivery, comparison, reviews, and persistent purchase controls. Distinction comes from how the product is examined.

### Direction B

**Guided confidence**: organize proof around the buyer's uncertainty. Use fit guidance, compatibility, provenance, configuration, service expectations, or booking constraints as the page narrative, then shorten the path to commitment.

### Decision variables

- Considered purchase versus quick replenishment.
- Number and complexity of variants.
- Trust gap, return risk, and regulated claims.
- Inventory, delivery region, booking time, and price transparency.
- Whether imagery is documentary, configurable, or aspirational.

### Motion ceiling

Low for cart and checkout; medium for product inspection, configuration, or a controlled reveal. Motion cannot delay price, availability, controls, or media inspection.

### Avoid when

There is no transaction, booking, subscription, or conversion state. A brand story with a single inquiry CTA usually belongs to the brand family instead.

### Verification focus

Variant and inventory states, image zoom and swipe, price truth, cart persistence, form errors, keyboard controls, checkout interruption recovery, and mobile purchase ergonomics.

## 4. 数据应用 / 仪表盘

### Representative sectors

Finance, healthcare, climate, logistics, education, research, operations, observability, public data, experimentation, and executive reporting.

### Default trap

Decorative KPI cards, unrelated chart types, color without semantics, dashboard density copied from a template, and a conversational AI panel added without a real data question.

### Direction A

**Decision cockpit**: organize the surface around one recurring decision. Establish current state, deviation, cause, affected entities, and available action. Charts and tables share filters and units.

### Direction B

**Analytical workspace**: optimize for exploration. Give users query/filter control, saved views, compare mode, provenance, definitions, and a stable detail panel. Let uncertainty and missing data remain visible.

### Decision variables

- Monitor, diagnose, compare, forecast, or report job.
- Data freshness, latency, confidence, and provenance.
- Number of dimensions and expected drill depth.
- Expert versus occasional users.
- Export, collaboration, alerting, and access-control needs.

### Motion ceiling

Low. Use transitions to preserve object constancy when filtering, sorting, expanding, or changing time range. Never animate values in a way that obscures exact reading or comparison.

### Avoid when

The data is only decorative proof on a landing page, or the primary job is narrative publication. A few metrics do not automatically make a dashboard.

### Verification focus

Units, axis and legend clarity, color semantics, empty/stale/error states, table overflow, filter persistence, keyboard access, loading stability, and consistency between summary and detail.

## 5. 内容 / 媒体

### Representative sectors

Editorial, journalism, photography, film, music, cultural institutions, research publication, documentation, archives, podcasts, and long-form brand content.

### Default trap

Magazine styling without editorial hierarchy, giant serif titles used as decoration, image walls without captions, scroll effects that interrupt reading, and identical cards flattening unlike content types.

### Direction A

**Reading system**: make hierarchy, line length, metadata, citations, navigation, and related context do the work. Use typography and spacing to distinguish article, note, archive, profile, and media transcript.

### Direction B

**Curated sequence**: treat the collection as an authored path. Use chapters, visual pauses, image/caption relationships, ambient media, or controlled horizontal sequences when order carries meaning.

### Decision variables

- Linear reading versus browsing an archive.
- Text/image/audio/video balance.
- Publishing frequency and taxonomy depth.
- Caption, attribution, citation, transcript, and search requirements.
- Whether chronology, topic, creator, or collection is the primary organizing idea.

### Motion ceiling

Low for reading; medium for transitions between chapters or media states. Scroll-linked effects must preserve selection, deep links, reduced motion, and reading position.

### Avoid when

Users primarily complete operational tasks, purchase products, or monitor live data. Content may support those jobs without defining the whole interface.

### Verification focus

Reading width, heading structure, captions and attribution, media controls, transcript access, deep links, focus order, print/share behavior, and no motion-induced loss of reading position.

## 6. 活动 / 沉浸式展示

### Representative sectors

Product launches, festivals, nightlife, sports campaigns, exhibitions, scientific visualization, generative art, games/esports, interactive installations, and time-bound cultural events.

### Default trap

Maximum spectacle without information hierarchy, neon or particles chosen by reflex, a 3D scene with no subject meaning, and essential dates, tickets, schedule, location, or accessibility hidden behind effects.

### Direction A

**Timed campaign**: use one high-impact visual system to create memory, then make date, place, schedule, access, ticket, and sharing paths immediate. The experience has a clear before/during/after lifecycle.

### Direction B

**Interactive phenomenon**: let a real concept drive the scene: sound response, physical simulation, spatial exploration, live data, game state, or audience input. Provide a static/reduced alternative that communicates the same idea.

### Decision variables

- Information urgency versus exploratory intent.
- Device/GPU range and expected network conditions.
- Whether interaction expresses the subject or only decorates it.
- Ticketing, schedule, location, age/access, and live-update requirements.
- Availability of real media and a meaningful fallback.

### Motion ceiling

Medium to high on the campaign or exhibit surface; low on ticketing, schedule, account, and accessibility paths. Budget one primary engine and a reduced/static equivalent.

### Avoid when

The experience is a daily operational tool, a dense analytical surface, a long reading task, or a checkout flow. Use this family only for the expressive layer, not the entire product.

### Verification focus

GPU and mobile fallback, loading progress, interaction discoverability, schedule/ticket visibility, reduced motion, audio controls, focus escape, text legibility over media, and nonblank canvas evidence.

## Cross-Family Overrides

同一行业可以进入多个任务族；以下是 task-first 的覆盖例子，不是固定映射：

- **医疗后台**：走 SaaS / 产品工具；临床数据监测可以走数据应用，而不是因为“医疗”就统一做成无菌品牌页。
- **生物科技发布**：走品牌 / 作品集或活动 / 沉浸式展示；研究数据分析工具则走数据应用。
- **时尚结账**：走 Commerce；时装周直播或系列发布可以走活动 / 沉浸式展示。
- **音乐节排期工具**：走 SaaS / 产品工具；音乐节宣传与现场互动才走活动 / 沉浸式展示。
- 酒店预订走 Commerce，酒店品牌故事走品牌 / 作品集，酒店运营后台走产品工具。
- 金融交易工作台走数据应用，金融机构品牌页走品牌，账户开户和付费方案可能走 Commerce。

## Anti-Stereotype Gate

Before committing to a direction, answer:

1. Did the family come from the job, or only the industry label?
2. What is the lazy visual default, and are we using it for a reason or by reflex?
3. Would a second direction produce a meaningfully different system, not a recolor?
4. Which existing assets, content, workflows, and constraints support the choice?
5. Does the chosen motion level fit the repeated task and device reality?
6. What evidence would make us switch directions?

If these answers are weak, do not hide uncertainty behind a persona name. Return to the brief, inspect the project, or ask one question that changes the path.
