# Visual Directions

Load this after identifying the subject, page register, audience, and available assets. Use it to choose a distinctive visual system without treating a named style as a preset.

## Contents

- Ambition profile
- Selection protocol
- Direction prototypes
- Hybrid rule
- Output contract

## Ambition Profile

Set three relative axes before choosing visual techniques. These are reasoning aids, not scores to expose to the user.

| Axis | Low | Medium | High | Guardrail |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| distinctiveness | familiar category grammar | one subject-specific signature | unmistakable authored world | identity must come from the subject, not novelty alone |
| spectacle | static craft and micro-feedback | one choreographed motion system | canvas, spatial, or cinematic engine | higher spectacle needs assets, performance budget, and a static equivalent |
| density | focused single task | balanced narrative or workflow | high-information operational surface | density follows the job, not the desired mood |

Register defaults are starting hypotheses:

- `brand`: medium-to-high distinctiveness; spectacle only when it clarifies the subject; low-to-medium density.
- `product`: medium distinctiveness through typography, tokens, and state behavior; low spectacle; medium-to-high density.
- `commerce`: medium distinctiveness around product truth; restrained spectacle; density changes by browse, compare, and checkout stage.
- `hybrid`: assign each route or region a register. Do not average incompatible requirements into one vague style.

## Selection Protocol

1. Extract subject evidence: material, process, place, data, vocabulary, product behavior, and usable media.
2. Name the likely lazy default, such as generic dark glass, interchangeable SaaS cards, or category cliche.
3. Choose two direction prototypes that differ in composition, type, material, and motion logic, not only color.
4. Test each direction against content hierarchy, interaction load, accessibility, implementation cost, and available assets.
5. Commit to one primary direction. Record the rejected direction and why it was weaker.
6. If evidence is thin, lower spectacle before lowering usability or inventing false brand detail.

## Direction Prototypes

These are adaptable design hypotheses. Never copy their surface treatment without translating it through the actual subject.

| Prototype | Useful When | System Ingredients | Motion Fit | Main Risk |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| cinematic technical | advanced hardware, engineering, infrastructure, scientific products | sharp type hierarchy, measured grids, close technical media, annotated depth | one spatial or media-led hero; precise state transitions | generic sci-fi darkness and unreadable overlays |
| phosphor operational | monitoring, diagnostics, observability, live systems | dense tables, restrained signal colors, monospace only for machine data, explicit status grammar | local updates, sweep or pulse only when tied to live state | turning every product surface into a terminal costume |
| editorial documentary | institutions, research, culture, place, human stories | strong headline/body contrast, captions, asymmetric pacing, truthful photography | paced reveals that follow reading order | decorative magazine layouts that weaken tasks |
| warm material | craft, food, hospitality, wellness, physical goods | tactile close-ups, quiet type, material-derived neutrals plus functional contrast | slow parallax or texture movement with static fallback | beige sameness and vague lifestyle imagery |
| precision luxury | considered objects, fashion, architecture, premium service | exact spacing, disciplined crop, restrained palette, high-quality detail media | minimal transitions, deliberate product inspection | confusing emptiness with premium quality |
| brutal typography | cultural campaigns, portfolios, launches with strong voice | oversized type used structurally, hard alignment shifts, direct copy, limited effects | type choreography as the single signature engine | sacrificing legibility or product comprehension |
| quiet spatial | portfolios, architecture, calm productivity, reflective tools | generous but purposeful space, layered planes, soft depth, stable controls | subtle continuity and depth; no constant floating | empty composition and low information scent |
| organic systems | climate, biology, gardens, care, circular systems | irregular but controlled rhythm, natural evidence, process diagrams, grounded color | growth, flow, or lifecycle metaphor | superficial leaf motifs and false sustainability cues |
| electric culture | music, events, youth media, experimental launches | expressive type, high contrast, controlled collision, authentic event media | beat-aware or sequence-led signature moment | continuous agitation and trend imitation |
| scientific generative | simulations, AI research, mathematics, complex models | parameter-driven forms, explanatory labels, visible cause and effect | canvas or WebGL only when interaction reveals the model | abstract particles with no explanatory role |

## Prototype Adaptation

For the selected prototype, define:

- **subject anchor:** the real material, behavior, or idea that makes the direction specific;
- **composition rule:** the repeatable spatial logic across the page or product;
- **type role:** what display, reading, label, and data text each do;
- **material rule:** how color, image, surface, and depth communicate hierarchy;
- **signature moment:** one memorable visual or interaction device;
- **restraint rule:** where the system becomes quiet so content and tasks remain usable.

Do not convert prototypes into fixed font, color, radius, or component prescriptions. A direction is successful when another subject could not inherit it unchanged.

## Hybrid Rule

A hybrid may use one primary prototype and one supporting prototype only when their roles are explicit. Example: editorial documentary for narrative pages and phosphor operational for a live-data workspace. Share semantic tokens and typography roles, but allow density and motion behavior to change by register.

Reject hybrids that merely accumulate effects. If two prototypes compete for the signature moment, choose one.

## Output Contract

Before implementation, state:

1. register and ambition profile;
2. selected direction and subject anchor;
3. rejected alternative and trade-off;
4. composition, type, material, and motion rules;
5. signature moment and static/reduced-motion equivalent;
6. the first visual assumption that must be verified in-browser.
